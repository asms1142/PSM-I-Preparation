Agile Manifesto Principles

1. Customer satisfaction comes first
Our main goal is to keep customers happy by delivering useful software early and continuously.

2. Accept changes, even late in the project
We welcome changing requirements because adapting to change helps customers stay competitive.

3. Deliver software frequently
We deliver working software regularly, preferably in short time periods (every few weeks).

4. Business and development teams work together
Business people and developers should communicate and collaborate every day.

5. Trust and support motivated people
Build teams with capable, motivated individuals, provide them with the right environment and support, and trust them to do their work.

6. Face-to-face communication is best
Direct conversation is the fastest and most effective way to share information within a team.

7. Working software shows real progress
The best measure of progress is having software that actually works.

8. Maintain a sustainable pace
Teams should work at a steady and manageable pace that they can maintain over the long term.

9. Focus on quality and good design
Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design makes it easier to adapt to changes.

10. Keep things simple
Do only the work that is truly necessary and avoid unnecessary complexity.

11. Empower self-organizing teams
The best solutions, designs, and architectures come from teams that organize and manage themselves.

12. Continuously improve
Teams should regularly reflect on their work, learn from their experiences, and make improvements.
